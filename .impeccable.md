## Design Context

### Users
International food importers from Europe, Asia, and the Arab world searching for a reliable Egyptian frozen food supplier. They arrive via Google or direct referral, need to quickly assess product range, quality standards, and how to get in touch. Mobile-heavy audience — many will browse on phones during trade shows or while comparing suppliers.

### Brand Personality
**Trustworthy, Fresh, Professional**
Al Noor is an Egyptian agricultural export company. The brand should feel like a premium food supplier — clean, confident, and grounded in nature. Not a tech startup, not a supermarket. Think: the pride of Egyptian agriculture meeting international trade standards.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Organic-professional. Deep greens from nature, golden accents for warmth and premium feel. Photography-forward — real product images do the selling.
- **References**: greentrade-egypt.com (similar industry), but elevated — better typography, more whitespace, stronger visual hierarchy.
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS landing pages, dark mode tech sites, neon gradients.
- **Theme**: Light mode only. Green palette tinted from nature (not neon). Golden/amber secondary for CTAs and highlights.

### Design Principles
1. **Photography first** — Real product images are the hero, not illustrations or icons
2. **Breathe** — Generous whitespace between sections; don't crowd content
3. **Trust signals** — Stats, certifications, professional layout build credibility
4. **Bilingual-native** — Arabic is not an afterthought; RTL must feel as polished as LTR
5. **Fast to scan** — Importers are busy; clear hierarchy, scannable sections, prominent contact info
